Research Focus

My doctoral research investigated the multifaceted role of silicon in enhancing plant resilience. I spearheaded comprehensive studies on how silicon supplementation helps critical crops like wheat and maize defend against a range of biotic and abiotic stressors.

Biotic Stress Tolerance

Investigated silicon-mediated defense against fungal pathogens like stripe rust, and insect pests including the corn leaf aphid and oriental armyworm.

Abiotic Stress Resilience

Analyzed the role of silicon in alleviating drought stress by enhancing physiological and biochemical stability in plants.

Advanced Biostatistics

Applied robust statistical models (ANOVA, MANOVA, PCA) using R to interpret complex biological data and identify novel stress tolerance markers.
